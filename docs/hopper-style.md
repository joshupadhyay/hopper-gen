# Edward Hopper Style Reference

## Defining Characteristics

### Lighting
- Dramatic directional light — usually from windows or artificial sources
- Strong contrast between illuminated and shadowed areas
- Side-lighting that sculpts forms geometrically
- Late afternoon / early morning "golden" tones, or harsh fluorescent interiors

### Composition
- Geometric precision — buildings, windows, walls create strong rectilinear frames
- Voyeuristic framing — scenes viewed through windows or from outside
- Strategic use of empty space to amplify isolation
- Strong diagonals (railroad tracks, roads, sunlight angles)

### Color Palette
- Muted earth tones: ochre, sienna, olive, slate
- Strategic saturated accents: red dress, green awning, yellow light
- Cool shadows, warm highlights
- Desaturated sky blues and grays

### Subject Matter
- Solitary figures or disconnected small groups
- Urban/suburban American settings: diners, gas stations, hotel rooms, offices
- Interiors with large windows, exterior light penetrating
- Quiet moments of introspection, reading, waiting

### Mood
- Psychological tension and quiet isolation
- Stillness — frozen moments of contemplation
- Urban loneliness even in populated spaces
- Cinematic quality — every scene tells an implied narrative

## Caption Strategy

- Every caption starts with "hopper style" as activation phrase
- Include: scene, lighting type, mood, color notes
- 15-30 words per caption
- Mix indoor/outdoor, day/night, figure/landscape
